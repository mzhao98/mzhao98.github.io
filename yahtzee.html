Alt-America: The Rise of the Radical Right in the Age of Trump

David Neiwert’s Alt-America: The Rise of the Radical Right in the Age of Trump details the history of the Alt-Right, a consolidation of numerous conservative groups, including conspiracists, white supremacists, and militiamen. These extremist groups had all been quietly operating throughout the nation prior to 2008, but after Barack Obama became elected president, violent crime and racist sentiment spiked dramatically. People who opposed immigration and gun control rallied with extremist groups, like conspiracists, militiamen, and white supremacists against the Obama Administration. Obama’s presidency played a pivotal role in igniting tensions among conservatives and causing the growth and coalescence of right-wing groups prior to Trump’s candidacy in 2016.

  • Designed a robotic arm using HEBI motors
  • Connected motors to ROS (robot operating system) nodes using C++
  • ROS is a robot operating framework that separates robotic parts into separate nodes. Each node can be controlled by subscribing and publishing to a centralized cloud of a commands.
  • Each node can either listen or send commands
  • Creates a Central Gameplay Controller node, a Robot Movement controller node, a Dice Detection Node, a Camera node.
  • Laser cut linkages between motors to act as the arms of the robot.
  • Implemented inverse kinematics to control the robot by specifying end position.
  • Used OpenCV, a computer vision library, to obtain the contours of the dice and located the circles on the dice.
  • Implemented Yahtzee gameplay logic in C++.
  • Movement Node: o The movement node utilized the inverse kinematics to command the robot to move to different locations in the workspace. The start and end point of the robot included interpolation points in order to move along the trajectory more smoothly.
  • Dice Detection Node: The dice detection nodes used the camera image to return the number of dice as well as their values in the workspace based on the pip count of the dice.
  • Gameplay Node: The game play node rolls the dice by placing them in a cup and then maneuvering the cup to dump out the dice. It keeps track of the Yahtzee score per player and takes as input user commands for the next plays.
  • Camera Node: The camera node interprets frames of the camera and locates the dice location.
